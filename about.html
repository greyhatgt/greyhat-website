Who We Are

GreyHat is the primary information security club at Georgia Tech. We are self-taught security enthusiasts who enjoy breaking things to find out how and why they work. Our goal is to teach the skills required to break things and the ethics necessary to wield them properly.

What We Do

Weekly Meetings

At our weekly meetings, we encourage student members, officers, alumni, and industry to come together and share the cool things they have learned or worked with.

Capture the Flag Competitions

We compete in capture the flag competitions throughout the year to hone our abilities and to train our security knowledge. Some CTFs are intended to be educational and others are competitive. We also provide weekly meetings to specially train students who wish to compete in these, as well as to create GreyHat's own annual CTF competition.

Resources

To further our members' knowledge, we provide as many resources as we can. We maintain a YouTube channel where all our talks are uploaded, and have a Wiki for reference. We also have CTF servers in the College of Computing to test and train our members with.

Industry Connections

Our vast and growing alumni network allows us to reach out to professionals who already have experience handling security in the "real world". These connections are valuable to us as they help us grow professionally and as a community.
